Magic in the West: My Experiences with Foreign But Mainly Somali Practitioners


Introduction:



Living in Britain for over twenty years since 2006, I have encountered several individuals who practice magic—some have even used it on me. My story reveals surprising encounters and observations about the forms of magic practiced by foreigners living in the West.
I. Common Forms of Magic Among Foreign Born Indivituals in the West
Personal Encounters: Birmingham Man From my arrival in Britain, I met people who openly admitted to knowing magic. Notably, the Birmingham man claimed to have learned magic in Egypt in 1980. And he advised me to cast spell on any woman i like to get her married otherwise he insisted i will never have a family and i vehemntly rejected. Usage of Magic: The primary reason these individuals approached me appeared to be to cast spells. Magic, in my experience, often involved subtle, mysterious acts rather than public displays. III. Reflections
Throughout these experiences, I have noticed a pattern of foreign men practicing magic—often for manipulation or personal gain. My research and observations suggest that magic based on scent, calling out the name of the victims's Mother and personal rituals may be prevalent among these mainly Somali Sorcerrors.
